Designed for fast context changes

Stop cycling through a hidden stack of windows.

Cmd+Tab is sequential. Mustache is spatial: every visible app gets a badge, pinned apps keep the shortcuts you expect, and noisy apps can stay out of the switcher.

Features

The demo is the product: press, see, switch.

01

Keyboard-first navigation

Use a global hotkey and one visible badge key to jump directly to the target application.

02

Readable overlay

Large app icons and sharp high-contrast badges make the next key obvious at a glance.

03

Search without leaving the overlay

Press Space, type a few characters, and press Return to jump to the first matching app.

04

Window picker

When the app has multiple visible windows, Mustache gives you a second compact picker for the exact target.

05

Flexible app sources

Choose whether to switch between all running applications or restrict it to apps in your Dock.

06

07

App exclusions

Hide distracting or rarely-used apps from the switcher while keeping pinned apps visible.

08

09

Track your app switching patterns and identify your most used shortcuts to optimize your workflow over time.

Workflow

A three-keystroke switch, no pointer travel.

  1. Invoke

    Press your configured hotkey, defaulting to Option + Tab, to show the app switcher overlay.

  2. Select

    While holding the modifier key, press the number or letter shown on the app badge.

  3. Search

    Press Space and type when you want to filter the overlay instead of scanning the grid.

  4. Land

    Pick a window if needed, or release the modifier to accept the first one, then Mustache brings the target forward.
